History of the “Cave Monsters” Game Development

Preface

Actual story of the game began later after the appearance of the first thoughts. We wanted to create not just a game, but an entire atmosphere, and for that we needed to imagine a game not only on the basis of “something new”. And creation of that “new” was taking from us lots of time and getting us away from the primary aim – creation of a good game.


“Everything new is well-forgotten old” perfectly suits this story. This game can hardly surprise you with its interface or control system. But it is that kind of a game, entering which, you find yourself being totally in the game. This is called the atmosphere and it is a very important point when creating a game. But here we have two sides of a medal again. On the one hand, there are games to be preferred while being in the subway or standing in a line, these are, as a rule, simple games like three in a row. On the other hand, when you come home, get yourself comfortable on a sofa and open this very atmosphere that we are talking about. I will tell you more about mobile development later in the article.


There is one serious point that was limiting us a lot - 2D design. Why? Here we can give several serious arguments. First one - mobile platforms. Creating a game for a year, then optimizing it for mobile platforms for a long time, and then cut off a serious number of audience (because of the insufficient power of the all the devices) became a reasonable point. Second – lack of a strong 3D designer and designer of appropriate textures. The first thing to occur to a fretful user is that 2D is something imperfect. But this thought instantly melts away when you look at such game legends as “Limbo”, which is on the picture below.


It was nominated for the best design among 3D graphics competitors, and this was inspiring me by the end of project. By the way, did you notice that it has only two colors – white and shades of grey? Will you find something new in the game? No. Will you be surprised by the new game control? No, but will you say that this game is a bad one? I think no, even if you did not play it. And here we get an important conclusion which is applicable not only to the sphere of game development, but to life in general. Nothing but your own imagination and creativity limits you. And I had a wish to give my fantasy a life…


Positive moment is the creation of a team. Since company had more than one programmer I had to get a companion for cooperative development. But in the end I volunteered to work alone which played a crucial role in the game design. On the one hand, team-work allows you to analyze your creations from a different point of view. But on the other one, working on your own provides your fantasy with no limits.


What to start with?


I have partially mentioned that the main complexity of work with mobile platforms is 2D design. Among other factors, which make the aim to drag the player into our reality more difficult, are small screens. And though it is not already such a great problem after 20 mins in the game, at the beginning it really is. Here appears a problem for designer. There should be lots of elements, but they should not drag attention from the main hero away. The importance of the background could not be overrated, though at the first sight it is not an element of primary importance.


Very soon I will return to the background, but for the fullness of picture I want to come up from the other side. Now I would like to ask you to imagine well-known game “Mario”. It is a typical platformer, when a hero stands on a platform - let it be ground. When you look at the threshold of the game, when he is running on such platforms, you begin to conscious one effect – the ground under him is not simply a picture, where he is moving. It is a special zone on which we build up a peculiar imagination of a ground in our fantasy, our fantasy moves away from the 2D model.


Moving on. Some elements, e.g. trees, are situated on the foreground in the reference to the hero. In such a way the hero is blanked out when passing through these elements, and again in our mind we create the comprehension of such objects that are not 2D for our fantasy and they begin to play the part of fully-valid things. This interplay of game world is very important for our atmosphere.


You can and you need to interact with the world in various ways. Each move of the hero should create more of such elements. That is why the background should become such an element of perception. When you walk along the street the motion of the trees in the background is not still or running with you, but slightly moving to your side. In the development this effect is called parallax.


As an example we can take the same street with buildings: trees on the background are moving with one speed, lamppost (which is also a part of the background) is moving with another and so on. In practice this looks something like on the picture below.

Each layer creates its own part in the user’s perception. The motion of clouds would be slow even if the hero is standing still, and fast, when he is going. The sun would shine brighter and greater if only it is not covered with clouds. The motion of mountains will be extremely slow and the land on the background would move a bit slower than the hero.


Such games as “Nightmare: Malaria” и “Anoxemia” are Android and iOS games that perfectly managed to create multilayer parallax. They are successively shown on the pictures.



After the short period of code implementation I already had resources to work with multilayer parallax. It was only the problem of design that stayed and which, clearly, I could not do. But the artist issue I will keep to the end.


I want to move this wall!


One more moment about which all the users are dreaming is the maximal world interaction. To move something you cannot move and to stroke something you cannot stroke. Maximal interaction is just what I wanted to create in the game. Movement of a hero makes the grass fluctuate under his feet. You could drag any brick by the road, shake a tree until it falls, to dig the soil in any possible place and so on. I wanted nothing to stop the hero and I knew how to do it. The only thing that suffered from such a total interact was the game size about which we will be speaking later. By using standard methods all these continued being highly-functioning and easy to access on mobile devices.


Total world interact allows to solve one and the same problem in different ways. Freedom of problemsolving! This is not throwing of birds in the same direction in order to destroy a construction. It is a throwing of trotyl-stuffed birds with jet engines and start-buttons right into a desired location. Do you feel the difference?


One more example that makes this moment hard to overestimate is called “Machinarium”. It is really incredible game well-known to all. Your aim in the game on its every stage is to find and solve the problem: to find the way out of the room, to reach the other side of a cliff by using all the means available. As a rule each problem has only one solving. But why not to get out of the room by crawling through the window, breaking or bursting the door open? To reach another side of a cliff by bulking lots of junk on its bottom or to jump it over with a pole?


I think I have succeeded in the explanation my aim. And all these were exactly what I wanted to implant in the game. It would be total freedom of action, limited by the level of the game, where each new level would open something uniquely new in the behavior or action of a hero.


Why the game weights more than 2 MB?


Now we reached another limiting moment in the process of game development for mobile platforms. At the same time game size is important and not. Not long ago game popularity was partially dependent from actual game size. And it is more likely that a user will download 10 games of 2 MB than 1 of 20 MB. And nowadays situation is the same and this factor is not going to lose its importance quickly. But as the technologies develop in a crazy pace we can get an indulgence. Speed of the internet in public networks (from 20 to 100 Mbit\s) today allows downloading games with huge sizes.


On the other side, is it possible for a good game with long and interesting story to be 20 MB? Not likely. And users partially begin to understand this.


Second technological moment – performance capabilities of new devices. Mobile performance is disputable and interesting moment and we will not touch it yet. Another thing is that phone memory opens us wide horizons. Capabilities of modern smartphones vary from 4 to 128 GB. Models with small internal memory (usually these are models with 4 to 8 GB of built-in memory) allow using external drives like small USB flash drives. All these give us the second indulgence: - modern (and not really) smartphones have quite big memory even for the storage of huge files.


I worked on it a lot and I want to tell about it

Now we are going to speak about light. Nobody argues that it is very important for 3D. More than that – I will not be speaking about it if I was not dealing with that sort of games. But is there something to tell about it in relation to 2D games?


To start with do you meet lots of 2D games that use light and especially on mobile devices? There are too little of them because it is the question of performance and many people just decide to discard it usually underestimating this moment. Pay your attention to the picture below:


Is not the comprehension of the cubes is building up here? The three-dimensional effect is being created and we feel their completeness. Our consciousness is not limited by the fact that it is just a picture on the screen. We enter this comprehension and this atmosphere. This is one more moment of development that took a lot of time to find the resources needed.

After more than one day of tries to create and reproduce something similiar (and there are a lot of such stuff on the internet) it became quite possible to make something like this. There is one big flaw represented on the picture above. As you can see all the shadows are sharp and not blurred. Another example of such poor shadow you can see on the other picture:


You may say that this problem is easily solved by adding a blurring effect on a certain layer (where the actual shadow is). But how will this filter influence game performance? Very deplorably I may say. So the next problem that I faced was a good camera shader to solve the problem. There were several solutions and I managed to find a good camera shader that blurred the shadow in the right way, but it also blurred the background on which the shadow was depicted. And it was absolutely not what I needed! This shader worked logically– shadow was depicted on a certain layer so the background is to be on that layer too.


Here, luckily, works of a very talented man came in handy. It was indie developer named Jake (to whom I am incredibly thankful) and his work entitled VLS2D Light. He answered all my questions with his developments the results of which I will show soon.


In conclusion


This is not a complete game. And I would really like to tell you about it being successful, but it is another story. The game did not find sustenance and there no time to make it perfect.

More than that, we decided that the main person in such a development is surely a designer. Only due to design, general plan, game idea you may enter the great world of game industry.

We have no game story or at least one resource made by designer. There is nothing, and everything that I used was simply found on the Internet. I would like to represent a game with bright future, but I cannot do it for this time.

There are only three screenshots of short work and great hope. Development took only a fortnight, but there existed a game idea for long years.
